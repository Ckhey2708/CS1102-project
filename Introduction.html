Introdcution to VR Fire Escape Simulation

  • Background

    • Recent advances in computer science have led to the widespread use of virtual reality (VR) technology in a variety of fields, including games, business, and education. Nowadays, virtual reality technology may replicate the real world and enable interaction through the use of external devices like headsets. Simulations give students the chance to better visualize ideas and allow them to observe processes that take place in ways that are invisible to the human eye (Holly et al., 2021). Another crucial element in the use of simulations and virtual worlds is interactivity with the course material. Virtual reality technologies provide highly interactive immersion by enabling intensive interaction with the virtual world.

    In order to raise awareness of fire safety in an entertaining and interactive manner, several CityU students created a virtual reality fire escape drill. Users can interact with realistic fire scenarios through the immersive experience created by virtual reality. Compared to traditional training approaches, this practical involvement helps learners retain information and skills with greater efficiency. This 'game' enables players to experience what it's feel being trapped during a blaze. To improve their emergency readiness, they might rehearse escape routes and decision-making techniques.

  • Application

    • Simulation trials are employed to build fire evacuation simulation instruction. Using immersive 3D technological devices, the simulated project replicates a fire scenario, and in order to finish the task, users can perform interactive actions throughout the entire fire escape drill.

    The virtual reality fire escape simulation serves as an instructional game. Participants can experience an integrated fire escape procedure in a virtual setting by means of the simulation. Users might encounter a fire alarm going off, learn the proper escape route, and use the fire apparatus to extinguish the fireâ€”which is difficult to access in everyday situations. VR simulations are more adaptable and easier to set up than actual fire drills. As a result, the drill can be conducted more often and, on more occasions, which advances society's understanding of fire evacuation processes.

  • Main functions

    • VR fire escape simulation aims to train users to familiarize themselves with the potential risks in extreme environments.

    First of all, users can comprehend various fire escape scenarios since virtual reality technology recreates the scene in real life. The goal of the fire escape simulation is to assist participants remain composed and make wise judgments in emergency scenarios by using virtual exercises. Safety concerns can be eliminated because the training are conducted in virtual reality settings.

    Second, users' responses to changes in extreme conditions are tested and practiced using VR technology. Users would have to navigate through a virtual building where they would have to spot dangers, find escape routes, and make snap judgments. Several difficulty levels encourage players to think more deeply, which improves their sense of danger and intuition. Additionally, these exercises assist users to become more accustomed to the escape process and improve their capacity to respond in actual fire escape scenarios.

    Third, more individuals might be drawn to the simulation and be more inclined to join provided a pleasant and engaging fire escape experience is planned. In the interim, the use of virtual reality (VR) lessens the need for physical presence and mobility during simulation, allowing users who are busy with work or incapacitated to participate in the virtual drill. Ultimately, increasing the number of participants and helping more people to become more conscious of the fire situation.

    Fourth, the simulation assists providers such as governments, institutions, and non-governmental organizations in assessing the capacity to evacuate the fire. Through data analysis, providers can determine users' deficiencies and enhance fire escape protocols. It is also possible to enhance the drill publicity and target audiences that lack fire awareness. Furthermore, virtual reality technology is more versatile than actual drills and can change environments according to the needs of the providers.
